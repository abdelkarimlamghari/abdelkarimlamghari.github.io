About me

I'm a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics with a focus on mathematical modeling and disease transmission. Based in Marrakesh, Morocco, I specialize in mathematical modeling, data analysis, and scientific computing. I am passionate about solving complex problems and applying mathematical models to real-world issues, particularly in the fields of public health and infectious diseases.

In addition to my research, I am a mathematics teacher with over six years of experience, dedicated to enhancing the learning experience for my students. I am particularly interested in the didactics of mathematics and its applications, focusing on making abstract mathematical concepts more accessible and engaging for students. I strive to integrate real-world applications of mathematics into my teaching, helping students appreciate its value and relevance in solving everyday problems.

My work involves developing models to understand the spread of infectious diseases, from micro to macro scales. I also collaborate on interdisciplinary research projects involving epidemiology, mathematical modeling, and statistical analysis. I am dedicated to making mathematical research both accessible and impactful in addressing critical public health challenges. My aim is to contribute to the advancement of public health through mathematical innovation and precision.
